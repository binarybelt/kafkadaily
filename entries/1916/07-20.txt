Have mercy on me, I am sinful in every nook and cranny of my being. But my
gifts were not entirely contemptible; I had some small talents, squandered
them, unadvised creature that I was, am now near my end just at a time when
outwardly everything might at last turn out well for me. Don't thrust me in
among the lost. I know it is my ridiculous love of self that speaks here,
ridiculous whether looked at from a distance or close at hand; but, as I am
alive, I also have life's love of self, and if life is not ridiculous its necessary
manifestations can't be either - Poor dialectic! 
If I am condemned, then I am not only, condemned to die, but also
condemned to struggle till I die. 
Sunday morning, shortly before I left, you seemed to want to help me. I
hoped. Until today a vain hope. 
And no matter what my complaint, it is without conviction, even without real
suffering; like the anchor of a lost ship, it swings far above the bottom in which
it could catch hold. 
Let me only have rest at night - childish complaint.
